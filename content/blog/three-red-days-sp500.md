---
title: "Three Red Days in the S&P 500 - Does It Work?"
description: "An educational overview of the three consecutive red days rule used by DipSignal."
date: "2026-04-27"
---

Three consecutive red days is a simple way to describe short-term downside pressure. DipSignal
defines the rule strictly: each of the last three S&P 500 daily closes must be lower than the
previous close.

The rule is intentionally basic. It does not measure magnitude, volume, or intraday reversals.
Instead, it asks whether the index has been persistently closing lower over several sessions.

By itself, three red days can happen often and may not be meaningful. Combined with elevated VIX,
extreme fear, or weak S5FI breadth, it can help identify moments when selling pressure is part of a
larger market stress setup.
