---
title: "VIX Above 30: A Statistical Buy Signal?"
description: "Why DipSignal treats VIX above 30 as a stress condition, not a standalone prediction."
date: "2026-04-27"
---

The VIX reflects expected volatility in S&P 500 options. A reading above 30 often appears during
periods when investors are paying more for protection and uncertainty is elevated. Historically,
those periods can produce interesting forward returns, but timing remains difficult.

VIX spikes can appear early in a correction, late in a correction, or repeatedly during a volatile
range. That is why DipSignal pairs the VIX rule with market breadth, sentiment, and recent S&P 500
closes.

When VIX is above 30 and at least one other condition is also active, the dashboard marks the
environment as statistically more favorable than normal. It is still only an educational indicator,
not financial advice.
