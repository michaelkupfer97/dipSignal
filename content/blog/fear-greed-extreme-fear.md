The Fear & Greed Index is popular because it compresses several market sentiment inputs into one
number. When that number falls below 10, the market is usually dealing with a strong fear regime.
That can happen during sharp corrections, macro shocks, liquidity stress, or sudden repricing of
earnings expectations.

Extreme fear does not mean prices must immediately rise. Fear can stay elevated, and markets can
fall further. DipSignal therefore does not treat this indicator as a standalone buy signal. It uses
it as one piece of a four-rule framework alongside volatility, breadth, and price action.

The educational value is in the combination. When extreme fear appears at the same time as high VIX
or weak breadth, it may describe a broader stress environment than sentiment alone.
